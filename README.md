# Market Research Analytics: Consumer Behavior & Ethical Marketing

## 📌 Project Overview
This project investigates the impact of deceptive marketing and influencer endorsements on consumer purchasing behavior within the skincare industry. The research focuses on analyzing consumer trust, financial decision-making, and brand credibility using primary survey data.

## 🎯 Objectives
* To measure the behavioral shifts of consumers when exposed to deceptive skincare product endorsements.
* To apply statistical analysis to primary data to uncover significant trends regarding consumer trust and purchasing likelihood.
* To provide data-driven recommendations for ethical marketing practices.

## 📊 Methodology & Tools
* **Sample Size:** 200 female respondents (Youth demographic, Ahmedabad).
* **Data Collection:** Structured primary survey (Google Forms).
* **Data Processing:** Microsoft Excel (Data cleaning, structuring, and pivot tables).
* **Statistical Analysis:** Jamovi. 
    * Conducted hypothesis testing including **Two-Proportion Z-tests**.
    * Utilized **Spearman's Rank Correlation** to measure the relationship between endorsement trust and purchasing habits.

## 📂 Files in this Repository
* **`Skincare_Survey_Data.csv`**: The raw and processed dataset containing the survey responses.
* **`Comprehensive_Research_Report.pdf`**: The full academic report detailing the methodology, Jamovi statistical graphs, and final conclusions.
* **`Project_Presentation_Slides.pdf`**: A high-level presentation summarizing the key findings and business recommendations.

## 💡 Key Business Insights
1. **Trust Deficit:** A significant correlation exists between the perceived authenticity of an influencer and the consumer's willingness to purchase, highlighting the financial risk of deceptive marketing for brands.
2. **Verification Behavior:** Data indicates a strong trend of consumers cross-referencing influencer claims with secondary reviews before committing to a purchase.
